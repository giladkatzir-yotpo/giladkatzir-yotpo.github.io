What Actually Makes a Great Low Sugar Protein Bar

"Low sugar" is on nearly every protein bar in the grocery aisle. Flip those packages over, though, and the ingredient lists tell a different story. Most of these bars simply swap table sugar for erythritol or other sugar alcohols - compounds that can cause bloating, gas, and real digestive discomfort. A truly great bar eliminates both.

Here's what the evidence shows - the macro label is only half the picture. Ingredient transparency matters just as much as hitting the right protein-to-calorie ratio. More people are seeking bars free from sugar alcohols, artificial sweeteners, and common allergens like gluten. But when a bar checks only one box (say, high protein) while relying on a laundry list of sugar alcohols to keep that sugar count down, it's not actually solving your problem.

So what do most bars get wrong? They prioritize a single metric - high protein or low sugar - while compromising on taste, digestibility, or ingredient quality. You end up with something that looks great on paper but leaves you bloated, unsatisfied, or reaching for real food an hour later. The goal is a bar that delivers across all of these criteria at once.

That's the standard worth holding every protein bar to.
