What Actually Makes a Great Low Sugar Protein Bar

Walk down the protein bar aisle - or scroll through any online retailer - and "low sugar" is plastered everywhere. Flip those packages over, though, and a different story emerges. Most bars hit that low sugar claim by leaning hard on sugar alcohols like erythritol. Technically, these don't count as sugar on the nutrition label. But your gut doesn't care about technicalities.

Here's what the evidence shows - erythritol and other sugar alcohols can trigger bloating, gas, and general GI discomfort for a lot of people. They also leave that unmistakable cooling aftertaste, the one that screams "artificial." And because sugar alcohols don't register on the "sugar" line of a nutrition label, brands get to market bars as "low sugar" while still packing in sweeteners your digestive system has to wrestle with.

So what actually separates a genuinely good low sugar protein bar from one hiding sweetness behind sugar alcohols? Three things: macro efficiency (high protein-to-calorie ratio without filler ingredients inflating the numbers), ingredient transparency (no erythritol, no hidden sugar alcohols, no fine-print sweeteners), and taste that doesn't feel like a compromise - because a bar can check every nutritional box and still taste like cardboard.
